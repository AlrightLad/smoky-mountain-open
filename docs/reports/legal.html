How this review works ↓

Parbaughs faces two kinds of legal exposure: app-store rejection (Apple/Google reject builds that miss specific guidelines) and real-world legal risk (a virtual-coin economy that strays into regulated gambling; privacy law; accessibility law). The single biggest exposure is ParCoins being construed as gambling — so it leads the checklist.

This page is evidence-based by rule. Each requirement names the law or store guideline, then cites the exact file and line that proves the current state. Where the evidence shows a gap that a human must close (a deploy gated by AMD-018, a paid asset, a business decision), it does not guess — it routes the item to the Founder Checklist with exact steps, and shows the link here.

Verdicts default to conservative: a requirement only earns green when the evidence is unambiguous. Anything uncertain is surfaced for Founder clarification rather than marked done. The review re-runs at every ship-close, so this page reflects the live state of the repo, not a one-time snapshot.

This is an internal orchestration surface. The two member-facing legal documents it audits are live at public/privacy.html and public/terms.html.

Compliance checklist — 7 requirements

1ParCoins are not gambling

Green — met
Requirement

A virtual currency becomes regulated gambling when all three of consideration (real money in), chance, and a prize of real-world value are present. Parbaughs must break at least one prong — ideally several — and say so plainly in its Terms. This is the platform's #1 legal exposure.

What we found

No real-money-in and no cash-out path exist anywhere in the code. ParCoins are earned by playing and spent only on cosmetic/social items inside the app. The Terms state this explicitly.

  • public/terms.html:20 — "ParCoins are a virtual, cosmetic-only currency with zero real-world cash value… cannot be redeemed, transferred, or exchanged for money, goods, or services outside the app… No gambling license is required because ParCoins have no monetary value."
  • Code grep across src/ — no stripe / paypal / cashout / real-money / in-app-purchase paths. Coin spend is internal only.
  • src/pages/shop.js, src/pages/richlist.js — purchases route through deductCoins for cosmetics (rings, banners, themes) and social actions; no fiat.
  • functions/ — no payment-processing code (the only "paypal" string is a transitive dependency's funding URL in package-lock.json, not app code).
Status: met. The consideration prong (real money in) and the real-world-value prong are both absent by construction, and the Terms assert virtual-only status. No action required. Re-verify if any earn/spend rule ever touches real money — that would flip this to red and is an AMD-018 gate-4 (payment economy) change.

2App Store & Play Store readiness

Yellow — human step
Requirement

Stores reject builds that miss specific guidelines: user-generated-content moderation (Apple 1.2 — report + block), in-app account deletion (Apple 5.1.1(v) / GDPR Art. 17), accurate privacy labels, and proper app icons. These gate submission, which the Founder has scoped as the final step.

What we found

UGC moderation is built and working. Account-deletion code is written, hardened, and emulator-verified, but the server function is not yet deployed (an AMD-018-gated human step). App icons are still a documented placeholder.

  • UGC report (Apple 1.2): src/pages/admin.js:413-441 — branded member-report flow writes to /reports and notifies the Commissioner; trigger at src/pages/members-detail.js:58. Comment names the guideline directly.
  • UGC block: src/core/utils.js:107-136 — each member stores a blockedUsers array; helpers add/read it.
  • Account deletion (5.1.1(v)): functions/index.js:267-347 — full reauth-gated, rate-limited self-erasure with audit log. But functions/index.js:260-262 notes deploy is AMD-018 gate 1; until deployed the endpoint 404s and the app routes the user to email support (fail-closed, Apple-acceptable interim).
  • App icons: public/manifest.json:16-23 points most sizes at watermark.jpg; public/icons/ holds only a generation README, no PNG icon set yet.
Status: met for the web/PWA product; two human steps remain before store submission. Both are routed to the Founder Checklist with exact steps: deploy deleteMyAccount (already queued, AMD-018 gate 1) and generate store app icons (DIY-or-designer). Neither blocks the current staging product; both are App-Store-submission-time, which is the Founder's designated final step.

3Privacy law (GDPR / CCPA / COPPA)

Green — met
Requirement

A published privacy policy must enumerate data collected, disclose third-party processors, honor data-subject rights (access, correct, delete, object), minimize collection, and address children's data. Location must be opt-in.

What we found

A current, comprehensive privacy policy is live and covers every required element.

  • public/privacy.html (updated 2026-05-28) — enumerates account/profile/golf/social/device data; states "we do NOT collect contacts, GPS tracking, or ad IDs" and "we do not sell your data."
  • Third-party processors disclosed: Firebase (US, SOC 2 / ISO 27001), GolfCourseAPI, Open-Meteo, Sentry (scrubbed).
  • Data-subject rights (GDPR/CCPA): access, correct, delete, object — with an in-app delete path and a 30-day support path.
  • Children's privacy: under-13 not intended, COPPA-aware; minimum age 13 set in Terms.
  • Location: opt-in, city-level, low-accuracy (documented + matches code behavior).
Status: met. One dependency to confirm: the data-subject-request and deletion-support paths both point to support@parbaughs.golf. Mailbox liveness can't be proven from code, so it is surfaced as a Founder clarification below.

4Terms of Service & liability

Green — met
Requirement

Published Terms must set a minimum age, define acceptable use, state the virtual-only nature of ParCoins, limit liability, and name governing law and a dispute-resolution path.

What we found

A current Terms of Service is live and covers every required element, including the load-bearing ParCoin clause that backstops item 1.

  • public/terms.html (updated 2026-05-28) — eligibility (invite-only, minimum age 13), user conduct, account termination.
  • public/terms.html:20 — ParCoin virtual-only clause (see item 1); internally consistent ("outside the app" qualifier correctly permits in-app wagers as virtual stakes).
  • Intellectual property clause: Parbaughs owns name/logo/design; user content remains the user's under a display license.
  • Limitation of liability ("as is"); dispute resolution by binding arbitration, York County, PA (AAA rules); governing law Pennsylvania.
Status: met. No edit required — the clause set is accurate against actual app behavior. Re-verify after any change to the coin economy or to who may transfer coins.

5Accessibility (ADA / WCAG 2.1 AA)

Green — met
Requirement

Consumer apps are expected to meet WCAG 2.1 AA: adequate touch-target size, visible keyboard focus, sufficient contrast, and screen-reader labelling. ADA exposure for public-facing apps is real.

What we found

The core accessibility primitives are present in the design system and a dedicated a11y ship has already landed.

  • 44pt touch targets: src/styles/components.css — min-height:44px on .back, .btn-sm, .feed-comment-input button, .cal-nav-btn, .cal-day, .advanced-stats-toggle, .miss-chip and more.
  • Visible focus: 28 :focus-visible / outline declarations across src/styles/base.css (13) and src/styles/components.css (15).
  • Shipped a11y work: v8.23.27 BL-008 band-aware drawer accessibility (WCAG 4.1.2 / 2.4.1 / 1.3.1).
Status: met. A formal end-to-end contrast audit across every token pairing remains a future polish pass (quality lift, not a blocker), tracked through ongoing design work rather than as a Founder item.

6Intellectual property & identity

Green — met
Requirement

The app must not misuse third-party marks, must license its external data sources, must respect user content ownership, and must offer a takedown path for infringing UGC.

What we found

Brand is original; external data is licensed; the Terms handle ownership; the report flow doubles as a takedown path.

  • public/terms.html IP clause — Parbaughs owns its original name/logo/design; user content stays the user's under a display license.
  • Course/par data sourced only from the licensed GolfCourseAPI (disclosed in public/privacy.html); no scraped third-party marks.
  • Takedown path: the member report flow (src/pages/admin.js:413-441) routes infringement reports to the Commissioner for action.
Status: met. No third-party trademark or copyrighted asset is embedded. Re-verify if any external logo, font, or media is ever added.

7Security as a legal duty

Green — met
Requirement

Privacy and breach-notification laws make reasonable security a legal obligation, not just an engineering one. Auth, data access, and audit logging must be sound; secrets must never be exposed.

What we found

Security is governed by operating principle P8 (ship-blocking) and the AgentShield scan; this reviewer defers to that posture and confirms the legally-relevant controls exist.

  • P8 (security + privacy + abuse-prevention) is ship-blocking: RED blocks, YELLOW needs Founder approval; every ship-close writes a SECURITY retrospective block.
  • Compliance audit trail: functions/index.js:324-336 writes a PII-free account_self_deleted entry before auth deletion, so erasure is provable for GDPR/store evidence.
  • Defensive controls on the deletion endpoint: CORS allow-list, bearer-token + checkRevoked verification, 5-minute reauth freshness, 5/min rate limit.
Status: met (owned by P8). Breach-notification readiness (who/when/how to notify) is a process item that becomes relevant only on a real incident; the data-handling disclosures in the privacy policy already set expectations.

Clarifications routed to the Founder

Is Parbaughs an individual or a business entity?

The Terms operate under the name "Parbaughs," and App Store / Play Store seller accounts require a named legal entity (an individual or a registered company such as an LLC). This is a business decision that cannot be read from code, and it affects the Terms' liability framing, the store seller account, and tax handling.

Routed to the Founder Checklist as legal-entity-clarification with options and steps. Not blocking the staging product; needed before store submission.

Is support@parbaughs.golf live and monitored?

The privacy policy, the Terms, and the account-deletion fallback all direct members to support@parbaughs.golf for data-subject requests and deletion. If that mailbox is not yet receiving and monitored, those compliance promises can't be honored. Deliverability can't be proven from the repo.

Routed to the Founder Checklist as support-email-liveness with a one-minute verification step. Surfaced rather than assumed, per the evidence-based rule.
