# Entity decision — Parbaughs operates as a BUSINESS ENTITY

**Decided by:** Founder, 2026-06-09 (chat batch approval).
**Founder's words:** "parbaughs is a business entity not a individual."

## Decision

Parbaughs operates under the framing of a **business entity (company)**, not a
named individual / sole proprietor. The Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
operate under the name **"Parbaughs"** as that entity.

## State of the Terms / Privacy (verified 2026-06-09)

`public/terms.html` already frames the operator generically and collectively
("Parbaughs is a golf league platform…", "we're a small team", "We are not
liable…") — i.e. **as a business/collective, never as a named individual or
sole proprietor.** The limitation-of-liability clause is entity-agnostic.

**=> No Terms/Privacy edit is required for this decision.** The current copy is
already consistent with "business entity."

## What remains a Founder step (money + identity — out of agent scope)

- **Formal registration.** "Business entity" is a framing decision; the agent
  has recorded it. If the Founder wants the actual legal liability shield and a
  store-seller-account-eligible registered company, a **PA LLC filing** (~$125,
  PA Dept. of State) is still a Founder money+identity step. Until then the
  entity is unregistered ("doing business as Parbaughs").
- **When it matters:** App/Play Store submission requires a named legal seller;
  and if the deferred cash-purchase ParCoin model is ever built, an LLC becomes
  materially more attractive (real-money flows → liability shield). No real-money
  flows today (ParCoins virtual-only), so exposure is low now.

## Follow-up if/when an LLC is registered

- Update the entity **name** referenced in `public/terms.html` /
  `public/privacy.html` to the registered LLC name.
- Consider adding a governing-law line (PA) and the registered entity name to
  the liability clause.
