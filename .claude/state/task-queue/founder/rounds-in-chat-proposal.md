# Proposal: round results in the Clubhouse chat (brief action #5)

**Status:** AWAITING FOUNDER taste call · written 2026-06-11 · AMD-015 propose-first
**Why this needs you:** it reverses a deliberate design decision you ratified.

## The tension

The market-strategy brief (action #5, "deepen banter") recommends rounds
auto-posting into chat — score posts are the natural conversation starters
("fair warning to the trees"). But the Clubhouse chat deliberately filters
ALL system/auto content today ("clubhouse is for human conversation only" —
chat.js snapshot filter), and round stories were intentionally routed to the
Feed instead. Both choices were good ones; they just point in opposite
directions for banter.

## Proposed answer (recommend: Option B)

- **A. Keep as-is.** Chat stays 100% human; the Feed remains the round
  surface. Zero work. Banter stays where it is.
- **B. Compact round chips, league-optional (recommended).** When a member
  finishes a round, a SINGLE-LINE quiet chip appears in chat ("⛳ Nick — 84
  at Honey Run · view"), visually distinct from human messages (no avatar
  header, muted, small), tappable to the round, with kudos inline. Default
  ON for new leagues, and a league setting (`settings.roundsInChat`) so the
  founding league can vote with its feet. Human-first reading is preserved
  because chips never stack headers and group like the new author-groups.
- **C. Caddy commentary.** The Caddy posts editorialized one-liners about
  rounds. Most fun, most noise; needs careful frequency caps. Could follow B
  later rather than lead.

**Recommendation: B.** It deepens banter where the people already are,
stays quiet, and the per-league toggle respects "community over
competition in every decision." C can ride on B's plumbing later if B
proves itself.

**Reply with:** "rounds-in-chat: A / B / C" (one word is enough) and the
team builds it next cycle.
